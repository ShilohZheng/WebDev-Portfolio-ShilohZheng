Applying the Design Thinking Process

To create a meaningful and user-friendly personal website, I followed the five stages of the Design Thinking process. My goal was to design a clean and beautiful space that showcases who I am and what I care about — especially my connection to the UN Global Goal 15: Life on Land.

  1. Empathize: I imagined how students and teachers might explore my site. I wanted them to feel that my website is clean and neat. I hope they can be interested, and able to learn about my values and personality with ease.
  2. Define: I identified my key goals: the website should reflect my interests (like travel and nature), explain my connection to Goal 15, and be visually appealing but simple to navigate.
  3. Ideate: I brainstormed layout ideas and jot down some ideas for how each section could look. I chose soft colors and clear fonts which is how I usually prefer when look at others' designs.
  4. Prototype: I built a draft of each page using HTML and CSS, tested different background shades, font sizes, and made sure the layout worked well across all devices.
  5. Test: I reviewed the site on my own and asked my friends for feedback. Based on their advice, I adjusted spacing, improved the font sizes, and made the colors more consistent.

Color Choices

I chose a warm-cool Morandi color palette with soft blues, purples, oranges, greens, and yellows to give a peaceful and elegant feeling. These tones also reflect nature and balance, which connect to my love for wildlife and the environment.

Layout and Typography

My layout focuses on clarity. Each page includes clear headings, soft spacing, and readable Times New Roman text. The layout is consistent across all pages, with a navigation bar, header, and footer. I reduced body text size slightly to keep everything neat and minimalist.

Overall Design Goal

My design motto is "simple and beautiful." I avoided clutter, used soft color blocks for visual interest, and let the content speak for itself. Every design choice connects back to who I am and what I want to share — especially my appreciation for nature and mindful living.
